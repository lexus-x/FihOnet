The Challenge

Traditional image classifiers fail in open-world ecosystems where new, unseen species appear. In the CV4Ecology 2026 challenge, images must be classified across many possible categories that include both Known Species (seen during training) and Unknown Species (novel classes not seen during training).

Text descriptions are provided for all species, enabling zero-shot recognition of unknown species once they are detected by an open-set gate.

The General Idea

  • Feature Representation: Encode each image with a strong biology-pretrained vision-language backbone.
  • Open-Set Gating: Use a confidence score (such as the Maximum Logit Score) to decide whether an image is a known or a novel species.
  • Known Classification: Route confident images to a closed-set classifier over the known species.
  • Unknown Classification: Route rejected images to a zero-shot text-matching head built from the provided species descriptions.

Note: this dashboard illustrates the concepts only. The training pipeline and methodology are maintained privately and are not part of this public repository.

1. Vision-Language Backbone

Open-set species recognition starts with a strong image encoder. Biology-pretrained vision-language models map an image into a normalized embedding that can be compared against both class prototypes and text descriptions — the foundation for both the known and the unknown routes.

2. Maximum Logit Score (MLS) Gate

Instead of relying on normalized Softmax probabilities (which suffer from overconfidence on novel classes), open-set methods often threshold the raw maximum logit output. The decision boundary is defined as:

$$\text{Decision}(x) = \begin{cases} \text{Route to Known Head} & \text{if } \max_{c \in C_{\text{seen}}} f_c(x) \ge \theta \\ \text{Route to Unknown Head} & \text{if } \max_{c \in C_{\text{seen}}} f_c(x) < \theta \end{cases}$$

The threshold $\theta$ is calibrated on a held-out split that simulates unknown species.

3. Known Classifier Head

Confident images are routed to a closed-set classifier over the known species. A common, lightweight approach is nearest-class-mean / prototype matching on top of frozen or parameter-efficiently fine-tuned embeddings.

4. Unknown Zero-Shot Head

For novel species, the image embedding is matched against text embeddings generated from the provided species names and descriptions — a zero-shot retrieval over the candidate vocabulary. Because the challenge provides text for every species, this route can recognize classes the model never saw during training.
